KASHI TOUR

ABOUT TEMPLE

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple in Varanasi is one of Hinduism's holiest shrines, dedicated to Lord Shiva as the Ruler of the Universe. Located on the Ganga's western bank, it houses one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, featuring a 250-foot shikhara with 800 kg of gold plating. The current structure was built by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar in 1780.Key Aspects of Kashi Vishwanath Temple:Significance: Known as the holiest Shiva temple, a visit and bathing in the Ganga are believed to lead to moksha.

It is a renowned Jyotirlinga, also considered a Shaktipeeth.History & Architecture: The original temple was destroyed and rebuilt multiple times. The present structure, built in 1780 by Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore, features a golden dome and spire. The nearby Gyanvapi well is where the main idol was hidden to protect it from destruction.Location: Located in the Vishwanath Gali of Varanasi, the temple is now easily accessible via the new Kashi Vishwanath Corridor, connecting the Ganga riverfront directly to the temple complex.

GHAT NIGHT

Varanasi's ghats at night are a mesmerizing blend of spirituality, light, and tradition. The riverfront transforms with the spectacular Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat, where thousands of lamps light up the night, thousands of diyas float on the water, and the old city lanes come alive.Key Experiences & AtmosphereGanga Aarti:

The main event at Dashashwamedh Ghat is a synchronized ritual with, music, and fire.Evening Boat Rides:
A serene way to watch the illuminated ghats, temples, and thousands of floating diyas from the water.Manikarnika Ghat (Burning Ghat):
A powerful, quiet spot, especially at night, where cremation pyres burn 24/7 as a symbol of the cycle of life and death.Night Walks:
Exploring the quiet, narrow, and historic streets (galis) and observing the evening life by the Ganges River.
